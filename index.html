What is GYTA


Global Youth Transformative Aid (GYTA) is a humanitarian, Non-Governmental Organisation (NGO) geared at solving the United Nation Sustainable Development Goals (UNSDGs) through partnerships with other National International NGOs and CSOs to better approach these human problems seeks to create a more sustainable future in the agricultural sector to provide food, quality education and decent self-employed jobs to over 10,000 poor rural families and 5,000 poor urban families by 2030 as a means to alleviate poverty hunger and poor striking communities through Entrepreneurship and livelihood schemes. GYTA has trained over 12,000 young persons in some 7 regions of Cameroon with about 5,000 youths and 1,000 rural farmers and supported over 700 youths and rural famers in the North West Region since 2014 GYTA is setting up training centres in some parts of the country starting from Bamenda-NWR. GYTA has set up agricultural and livestock training centres such as poultry, piggery, rabbitry, fishery, feed mill and GYTA vocational training institute to empower underprivileged youths to be able to put food on their tables. Setting up branches in Yaounde-CR and Buea-SWR are already on the pipeline.


Poverty stands as the major barrier factor for development and growth of every citizen and their communities. Most African countries are being raked by poverty hence, making it difficult for upcoming agripreneurs to think well, acquire some seeds, education, food items and other basic needs, necessary for their development. GYTA has played a major role by empowering these youths in most rural parts of the country with skills to create and bring their dreams into reality.
